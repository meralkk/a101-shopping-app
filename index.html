Pain Point 1: Can't see products without being a member

Some users just want to see the products, compare prices, and navigate between categories.
According to the current original app, the user must complete a 12-step subscription just to view the products.

Solution for Pain Point 1

Users had to be a member to view products in the current application. In this way, according to their location, it could be checked whether there was service to the region, and information about the stock situation could be given. However, this situation prevented those who only wanted to see the products and want to compare prices without being a member.


My solution here is to enable the user to view prices, products, and other details without being a member. After downloading the application, users can access the home page and browse the products without sharing the location and without being a member.

Design for Pain Point 1

Here, the user can reach the home page after a few steps to view the products.

Also, when users add a product to the basket and want to go to the order phase, a reminder to share their location may appear. The user can see whether the products in the basket are distributed to the region where they are located, by selecting their address with a single click.
According to the current original app, the user must complete a 12-step subscription just to view the products.

Pain Point 2: I'm a member now but no service to my address!

Some users just want to see the products, compare prices, and navigate between categories.
According to the current original app, the user must complete a 12-step subscription just to view the products.

Solution for Pain Point 2

Here, after users enter their own locations, if there is no service for their region, various alternatives can be offered to them.


When the delivery begins to the region where the user is located, they can be notified by email, they can view the list of delivery areas or create an order by entering another address.


In this way, users can be offered different solutions instead of leaving the application with a bad experience with a single message.

Design for Pain Point 2

In this new design, I aimed a direction that gives more informational messages than a classic banner warning.

Pain Point 3: I am having trouble searching and finding products

There is no search box on the homepage and it is difficult for the user to search only in the categories section.

Solution for Pain Point 3

The search box added to the homepage allows products to be searched by categories and products. It also provides product search facilities such as sorting filtering.

Design for Pain Point 3

I made the new design by editing the deficiencies in the current application. On the side screens, there is the design of the current application and the new design that emerged after the new arrangement.
